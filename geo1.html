Brief Points

Topographical Sheet
A toposheet (topographical sheet) is a detailed, large-scale map that provides a precise, two-dimensional representation of a three-dimensional landscape. It acts as a comprehensive record of both natural features (rivers, forests, hills) and man-made features (settlements, roads, railways).
Atmosphere
The atmosphere is a dynamic, multi-layered blanket of gases—mainly 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen—surrounding Earth. It protects the planet from solar radiation and extreme temperatures while sustaining life by supplying oxygen and creating weather.
Weather and Climate
Weather refers to short-term, day-to-day atmospheric conditions (rain, temperature, wind). Climate is the long-term average of these patterns over 30 years or more. Simply: weather is what you get; climate is what you expect.
Weathering and Soil Formation
Weathering is the breakdown of rocks into smaller particles through physical, chemical, or biological processes. This acts as the foundation for soil formation, combining minerals with organic matter, water, and air over time.
