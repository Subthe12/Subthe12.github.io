Fractions

  • A fraction represents a part of a whole or any number of equal parts.
  • It consists of a numerator (top number) and a denominator (bottom number) separated by a line.
  • The denominator shows how many equal parts the whole is divided into, while the numerator indicates how many parts are being considered in total.
  • Example of fraction, 3⁄4. 3 is the numerator, 4 is the denominator.
    It means that, out of 4 parts 3 parts are considered.

    • Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fractions : Proper fractions are fractions with the denonimator greater than the numerator.
    Example, 3⁄4 , 8⁄90, 2⁄5 , etc.
  • Improper Fractions : Improper fractions are fractions with the numerator greater than the denonimator.
    Example, 6⁄8 , 2⁄120 , 8⁄37 , etc.
  • Mixed Fractions : Mixed fractions are fractions which are a combination of a whole number and a proper fraction.
    Example, 53⁄4
  • Unit Fraction : Unit fractions are fractions with the numerator exactly 1
    Example, 1⁄4, 1⁄9 , 1⁄3 , etc.
  • Like Fraction : Like fractions are a group of fractions with the same denominator.
    Example, 9⁄4 , 5⁄4 , 2⁄4 , 1⁄4 ;
    8⁄24 , 58⁄24 , 9⁄24 , 32⁄24 ;
    19⁄40 , 94⁄40 , 74⁄40 , 17⁄40.
  • Equivalent Fractions : Equivalent fractions are different fractions with same value when simplified.
    Example, 1⁄2 and 4⁄8 are equivalent fractions as when we simplify those fractions we get a value of 1⁄2. (Simplification will be taught by us later in this webpage)
  • Decimal Fractions : Decimal fractions are fractions with a number in denominator which is a power of 10.
    Example, 12⁄10 , denominator is 10, 101. 14⁄1000, denominator 103.
